Research Design

The study uses quantitative and descriptive design methods. The study aimed to measure the impact of carbon credits on greenhouse
gas emissions in different regions and sectors, concerning the research question which is: How effective are carbon credits in reducing greenhouse gas emissions? To examine the effects of carbon credit trading on the environment and the economy. To determine the difficulties and impediments that businesses have when putting carbon credit schemes into action. To offer suggestions for maximizing the application of carbon credits as a climate change mitigation instrument. The research hypothesis posits that carbon credits significantly and positively contribute to reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
The research variables are:
1. Independent variables: - Factors that affect greenhouse gas emission such as forest area and Renewable energy consumption.
2. Dependent variable: - Greenhouse gas emissions measured by the total emissions of carbon dioxide in each region or country.

model

The model is based on multiple regression which takes the form:
\( y_i = \beta_0 + \beta_1 x_1 + \beta_2 x_2 + \epsilon \)
\( y_i \) - Response variable
\( x_1 \) - Independent variable 1
\( x_2 \) - Independent variable 2
\( \beta_0 \) - Intercept
\( \beta_1 \) - Coefficient for \( x_1 \)
\( \beta_2 \) - Coefficient for \( x_2 \)
\( \epsilon \) - Error term

OLS Method to estimate our parameters \( \beta_0 \), \( \beta_1 \), and \( \beta_2 \) which assumes \( \sum_{i=1}^{n} \epsilon_i = 0 \) and \( \sum_{i=1}^{n} \epsilon_i^2 \) is minimal.

The model: \( y_i = \beta_0 + \beta_1 x_1 + \beta_2 x_2 + \epsilon \) \( \epsilon_i = y_i - E[y_i] \implies \epsilon_i^2 = (y_i - E[y_i])^2 \)

\( \sum_{i=1}^{n} \epsilon_i^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_1 - \beta_2 x_2)^2 \)

Differentiating with respect to \( \beta_0 \): \( \frac{\partial}{\partial \beta_0} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \epsilon_i^2 = -2 \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_1 - \beta_2 x_2) \)

Making \( \beta_0 \) the subject of the formula: \( \sum_{i=n}^{n} \beta_0 = \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i - \beta_1 \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_1 - \beta_2 \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_2 \) \( \beta_0 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i - \beta_1 \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_1 - \beta_2 \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_2}{n} \) \( \hat{\beta}_0 = \overline{y} - \hat{\beta}_1 \overline{x_1} - \hat{\beta}_2 \overline{x_2} \)

From the equation (1.6) we have: \( \sum_{i=1}^{n} \epsilon_i^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_1 - \beta_2 x_2)^2 \)

We use parameters \( \beta_0 \) and \( \beta_1 \) and \( x_1 \) to get the model: \( y_i = \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_1 \)

Differentiating with respect to \( \beta_1 \): \( \frac{\partial}{\partial \beta_1} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \epsilon_i^2 = -2 \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_1)x_1 \)

\( \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_1)x_1 = 0 \)

\( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_1 - \beta_0 \sum x_1 - \beta_1 \sum x_1^2 = 0 \)

Substituting \( \beta_0 \) by \( \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i}{n} - \beta_1 \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_1}{n} \) for the model \( y_i = \beta_0 - \beta_1 x_1 \) we have: \( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_1 - \left( \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i}{n} - \beta_1 \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_1}{n} \right) \sum x_1 - \beta_1 \sum x_1^2 = 0 \)

\( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_1 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_1 \right)/n - \beta_1 \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_1^2 \right)/n - \beta_1 \sum x_1^2 = 0 \)

Putting the like terms together: \( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_1 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_1 \right)/n - \beta_1 \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_1^2 \right)/n - \beta_1 \sum x_1^2 = 0 \)

Making \( \beta_1 \) the subject of the formula we have: \( \beta_1 \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_1^2 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_1^2 \right)/n \right) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_1 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_1 \right)/n \)

\( \beta_1 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_1 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_1 \right)/n}{\left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_1^2 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_1^2 \right)/n \right)} \)

Putting \( x \)'s and \( y \)'s together we have: \( \beta_1 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} \left( y_i - \overline{y_i} \right) \left( x_{i1} - \overline{x_1} \right)}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} \left( x_{i1} - \overline{x_1} \right)^2} \)

We use parameters \( \beta_0 \) and \( \beta_2 \) and \( x_2 \) to get the model: \( y_i = \beta_0 - \beta_2 x_2 \)

Differentiating with respect to \( \beta_2 \): \( \frac{\partial}{\partial \beta_2} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \epsilon_i^2 = -2 \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left( y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_2 x_2 \right)x_2 \)

\( \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left( y_i - \beta_0 - \beta_2 x_2 \right)x_2 = 0 \)

\( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_2 - \beta_0 \sum x_2 - \beta_2 \sum x_2^2 = 0 \)

Substituting \( \beta_0 \) by \( \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i}{n} - \beta_2 \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_2}{n} \) for the model \( y_i = \beta_0 - \beta_2 x_2 \) we have: \( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_2 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_2 \right)/n - \beta_2 \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_2^2 \right)/n - \beta_2 \sum x_2^2 = 0 \)

Putting the like terms together: \( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_2 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_2 \right)/n - \beta_2 \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_2^2 \right)/n - \beta_2 \sum x_2^2 = 0 \)

Making \( \beta_2 \) the subject of the formula we have: \( \beta_2 \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_2^2 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_2^2 \right)/n \right) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_2 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_2 \right)/n \)

\( \beta_2 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_2 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i x_2 \right)/n}{\left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_2^2 - \left( \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_2^2 \right)/n \right)} \)

Putting \( x \)'s and \( y \)'s together we have: \( \beta_2 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} \left( y_i - \overline{y_i} \right) \left( x_{i2} - \overline{x_2} \right)}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} \left( x_{i2} - \overline{x_2} \right)^2} \)

Using this model, we checked for some assumptions of multiple regression such as independence of errors, homoscedasticity, linearity, normality of residuals, and multicollinearity between our independent variables \( x_1 \) and \( x_2 \).

Since the study relies on publicly available secondary data, ethical considerations are minimal.
Principles of respect, integrity, and beneficence will be observed, ensuring proper use of citation
and honest analysis of the data.
